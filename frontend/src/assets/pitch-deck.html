Turn Any Hard Textbook into an Easy Learning Journey

Learner converts dense books into guided, chunked, quiz-backed paths for students who feel left behind by traditional textbook learning.

Focus: Maths, Chemistry, Biology Audience: Tier 2/3 students in India Experience: Book to roadmap in minutes
Problem

Learning from books feels impossible when complexity and language barriers collide

  • Huge textbooks create cognitive overload before students even start.
  • Subjects like Maths, Chemistry, and Biology are difficult to self-parse from dense text.
  • Many Tier 2/3 students in India struggle with non-native textbook language.
  • Students default to random YouTube videos instead of structured book learning.
Core Insight

Students do not avoid effort. They avoid overload.

Splitting a big textbook into smaller, clear units instantly lowers anxiety and increases the chance that learners continue.

Huge book -> manageable chunks -> consistent progress.
Solution

An AI learning layer on top of any textbook PDF

  • Upload textbook PDF once.
  • Auto-create chapter and unit roadmap from table of contents.
  • Generate unit quizzes with explanations.
  • Attach focused video resources to each unit.
Product Flow

Simple, guided student experience

1. Upload

Student uploads a textbook PDF and gets an instant structure.

2. Roadmap

Book becomes chapter and unit journey, not a wall of pages.

3. Practice

Each unit includes quiz-based reinforcement and explanation loops.

4. Progress

Completion tracking keeps momentum and highlights what is pending.

Initial Market

Start where the pain is sharp and frequent

  • Tier 2/3 university students in India.
  • Concept-heavy subjects with high dropout in self-study.
  • Learners who currently stitch together random YouTube content.
  • Students seeking clear sequencing and measurable confidence gains.
Business Model

Per-book pricing (still being explored)

  • Primary hypothesis: charge per processed book, not monthly subscription.
  • Price band can map to book length and processing depth.
  • One-time payment matches semester-based student behavior.

Pricing structure and unit economics are still being explored through user testing.

Why We Win

Structured learning, not random content hopping

  • Book-first workflow keeps learning anchored to curriculum sequence.
  • Chunk-level quizzes close the understanding loop quickly.
  • Video support is contextual to unit intent, not generic search.
  • Progress visibility builds discipline and continuity.
